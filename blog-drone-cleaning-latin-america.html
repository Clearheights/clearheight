The Problem with Traditional High-Rise Cleaning

Let us be direct. Scaffolding-based and rope-access cleaning is dangerous, slow, expensive, and disruptive. In markets where labour is relatively cheap — like much of Latin America — the cost argument is sometimes used to justify continuing the old way. But cost per hour of labour is only one variable in the equation.

  • Safety liability: Every worker deployed at height is a liability. Near-misses are underreported. Fatalities are not unheard of. The human cost alone should be reason enough to seek alternatives.
  • Speed: A traditional scaffolding setup for a mid-rise building can take one to two days before cleaning even begins. A drone operation is airborne within minutes.
  • Disruption: Scaffolding blocks entrances, obstructs signage, and creates noise and chaos for tenants, customers, and visitors — a reputational and operational problem for hotels, retail, and offices alike.
  • Chemical residue: Traditional cleaning chemicals leave toxic residues on surfaces, in drainage systems, and in the surrounding air — a growing concern for ESG-conscious property owners.
  • Frequency: Because traditional cleaning is so costly and disruptive, most buildings are cleaned far less often than they should be — accelerating facade deterioration over time.

What Is Ionized Water Drone Cleaning — and Why Does It Work?

When most people hear "drone cleaning," they picture a consumer quadcopter with a garden hose taped to it. The reality is considerably more sophisticated — and the results speak for themselves.

Our system uses a professional-grade, tethered heavy-lift drone equipped with a precision nozzle and an integrated ionized water delivery system. The drone is operated by a single trained technician on the ground. There is no one on the facade. There is no rope. There is no scaffold. The only thing touching your building is water.

Why ionized water specifically?

Ionized water — sometimes called electrolyzed water — is water that has been treated to alter its electrical charge. This gives it properties ordinary water simply does not have:

  • Cuts through grease and grime at a molecular level, without the need for harsh chemical detergents.
  • Disinfects surfaces — ionized water neutralizes bacteria and pathogens on contact, making it particularly valuable for healthcare facilities, hotels, and food-adjacent properties.
  • Leaves zero toxic residue — it reverts to ordinary water after application, with no chemical runoff and no hazard to people in or around the building.
  • Delivers a streak-free, fast-drying finish — the molecular structure sheets off glass cleanly, without the streaking that soap-based systems commonly produce.

Why Latin America — and Why Now

Drone cleaning is already established in parts of Asia, the United States, and Europe. In Latin America, it is almost entirely untapped. That is not a weakness — it is a window.

The argument that cheap labour makes drone cleaning unnecessary misses the bigger picture. Yes, manual labour costs less per hour in Mexico or El Salvador than in Germany or California. But that calculation ignores several critical factors:

  • The total operational cost — including scaffolding hire, insurance, disruption to tenants, and time lost.
  • The liability exposure — one serious injury on a job site can cost a building owner far more than years of drone cleaning contracts.
  • The appearance economy — as Latin American cities grow and attract international investment, the standard for commercial property presentation is rising rapidly.
  • The sustainability pressure — ESG requirements are reaching Latin American commercial real estate. Chemical-free, low-disruption maintenance is becoming an expectation.
